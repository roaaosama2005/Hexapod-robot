Hexapod Robot

"Six legs, infinite possibilities"

A high-performance six-legged robot equipped with live-streaming cameras and advanced motion control. Designed for exploration, surveillance, and educational robotics experiments.

Learn More

About the Project

This Hexapod Robot is designed to move with insect-like grace using six individually actuated legs. Each leg has multiple joints, enabling complex motions. It’s controlled by a microcontroller (STM32F103C8T6 (Blue Pill)) and features wireless communication and live video streaming.

It is built for rugged terrains and stability, combining mechanical design with intelligent control algorithms to navigate uneven surfaces smoothly.

Features

Smart Control

Utilizes gait algorithms to coordinate leg movement and maintain balance.

Live Streaming

Integrated camera for real-time video feedback.

Adaptive Balance

Self-adjusts posture to stay stable on uneven terrain.

Modular & Expandable

You can add sensors like ultrasonic, IMU, or Lidar modules.

Main Components

STM32F103C8T6 (Blue Pill)

The brain of the robot — controls all 18 servo motors with high precision and ensures smooth movement through advanced algorithms.

ESP32-CAM

The eyes of the robot — captures live video and streams it directly to your device, enabling real-time vision and potential AI upgrades.

PCA9685 Servo Driver

The muscle controller — expands STM32’s capacity to control 18 servo motors simultaneously and efficiently.

HC-05 Bluetooth Module

The wireless bridge — connects the robot to your smartphone, enabling full control through the mobile app.

How It Works

Smart Motion with STM32

Coordinates six legs to move like a real insect — stable, balanced, and smooth in every step.

Live Vision with ESP32-CAM

Streams real-time video through Wi-Fi, letting you see exactly what the robot sees.

Wireless App Control

Control direction, speed, and camera view from your phone with a single touch.

AI-Based Upgrades (Future Ready)

The camera can detect obstacles or track objects using AI, making it semi-autonomous.
